Work in Progess

A portfolio about progress, not perfection

Hello! I’m Andy.

I'm a self-starter technology, art, and ideas addict who blends light shows, AI, dance, painting, and much more into my creative work. My projects start with an interesting idea, which inspires some research, and then imagination and learning sweep me away into adventures. The process for building art with technology has been like painting, full of explorations, changes in directions, leading to something I never expected when I started.

I’d love to turn my passion into a career by becoming a creative technologist. People are telling me that the best way to start is by making a website, and yet when I look at the polished portfolios of seasoned creative professionals, it feels intimidating to build. Rather than not start at all, I’ve decided to make a one page blog, call it a work-in-progress, that is, a launchpad for better things to come (link to threeJS animation Pop-up).

I’ve been an artist my whole life, but didn’t start blending the process with tech until last year. Here is my first Arduino light project from January 2021:

Even though my eight-year-old cousin was building cooler things with his Arduino, I shared this with everyone I knew. The more I learned, the more I saw the possibilities. What if I used a supersonic sensor to control an LED light? I completed that project in July 2021:

I remember having to write linear equations to make the transition smooth between the Red, Blue, and Green pixels, and feeling amazement that I was feeling enjoyment while doing math. Here is my latest iteration of the project:

This is a video frome my webcam which uses open source, motion-capture AI software in python to send hand or body position data to a microcontroller (ESP32). A C++ program analyzes the data, creates wild patterns, and sends it to the light strip. Other features include saving and accurately replaying your lightshows, options for other cool light patterns, control dynamics, color palettes, and data smoothing to reduce glitchiness. I will be doing a performance with it in March 2022. It’s at a talent show, but you gotta start somewhere.

Besides adding more patterns and control features, such as layering and editing, the goal is to build an web and iOS app that streams the data through the ESP32’s bluetooth with a social media feature for sharing light shows between friend’s light strips, syncing them to music. It sounds like a lot (it is), but I have done the research and it is totally possible! Here is the app in progress, which can currently connect to and send data to bluetooth and ead body position:

I’ve also been learning how to do projection mapping projects with Resolume, blending my love for light, dancing, painting, and music. My goal is to use these mediums to tell a story about wat it feels like to dance and share with others. The project will be complete when I can share it with others.
