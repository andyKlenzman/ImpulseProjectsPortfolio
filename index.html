LEDs & Me

A story about owning a Pet Rainbow

Hello! I’m Andy.

Probably like you, I'm that person always embarking on creative adventures. “Adventures” is really the only way to describe it. When I’m starting a new painting, it feels like I’m rappelling down an Indiana Jones-style cave with abstract arrows shooting out the wall, colors coalescing into trap doors, and ideas leading me to dead ends or bizarre holy grails.

My creative practice is all about following my curiosity, imagination, and wonder, regardless of where it takes me. Last year, it took a turn that I did not expect, but one which I’d been secretly yearning for since I was a kid. I used to gut old radios and clocks to admire the beautiful and mysterious electronics inside. Technology fascinated me, but understanding it seemed like an unattainable dream, a project for left-brainers. But when my uncle showed me the Arduino he bought for my little cousin, it reignited my curiosity and I bought one for myself, figuring that if a child could do it, so could I. Here is my first project completed in January 2021:

Even though my prepubescent cousin was building cooler things with his Arduino, I was amazed that I'd just understood and built something which used to seem so esoteric. Exploring technology was a similar experience to painting; the more techniques I learned, the more the creative potential unlocked. My Arduino kit came with a sensor measuring distance by the speed of sound, and I realized I could use the distance of my hand to control an RGB LED. I finished the project in July 2021. As you can tell by my shirtlessness and the adoring look on my face, I was very close with my “Pet Rainbow” :

My love for hardware was growing as fast as my love for software. I learned Python and how to use a machine learning library called OpenCV for tracking hand and body position. Watching my webcam track my fingertips made me wonder if I could use this new and improved data source to transform Pet Rainbow into a dance partner. The idea has spawned an adventure that continues to enchant and challenge me, and evokes the entire range of human emotions, from weeping-on-my-keyboard levels of burnout to direct-contact-with-god moments of revelation. Here is a maxed-out version of Pet Rainbow in March 2022:

A Python program sends my hand coordinates to an ESP32 microcontroller running a C++ program, which uses math to translate coordinate data into wild patterns, then pushes them out realtime to a 150 RGB SK9822 LED strip. Pet Rainbow and I will be doing our first live performance in March 2022, so we are training hard to pull off some new tricks.

Current features include:

  • One hand / two hand / full body data capture
  • Recording and replaying light shows for sharing with friends and complimenting the music you were dancing to
  • Selections for different light patterns, control dynamics, and color palettes
  • Data manipulation for analyzing, editing, and enhancing light shows

I’m working on more editing and layering options so you can craft lightshows with controls similar to a music looper, except the interface is controlled by dance rather than a pedal. I’d also like to turn Pet Rainbow into a web and iOS app capable of 1) capturing and streaming data wirelessly and 2) social media features for sharing light shows with other LED strips. Everyone I know is getting a Pet Rainbow for Christmas, so my goal is to finish the project by the end of the year.

Gutting radios and playing with LEDs has spawned so many new interests. If you mapped them out, the terrain would look like a giant tree branching into more trees. It would be too long of a post to explain all of them, so here is a list of my technology-inspired projects from January 2021, with some visuals and links to GitHub and sources of inspiration:

  • Video Jockeying, Projection Mapping, and Painting Fusion projects with Resolume
  • Sculpture with lights, motors, and random objects (ask me about my depressed sock puppet idea andyklenz@gmail.com)
  • Web design with HTML/CSS/PHP/Bootstrap (I made the website you are on)
  • Social media platforms with Firebase and Angular (inspiration to build with social media comes from my international friend Ben who writes the blog Tiny Projects. He created an awesome social media website called sNormal
  • 3D animation with ThreeJS/Unreal Engine/Blender (the website is going to look sick once I figure out how to add this stuff to it)
  • iOS Apps with Swift: besides building more Pet Rainbow features, I’d like to build an app called Quote Collector for collecting, sharing, and receiving quotes of inspiration
  • Video games with Python/OpenCV
  • Data Science with Numpy Pandas/Python (Data science is surprisingly creative, most of Pet Rainbow is designing and analyzing data structures)

The past year has shown me what’s possible when curiosity, imagination and wonder are your guides. Even though I had no clue where they were taking me, giving up control allowed for experiences, passions, and connections I never expected. I hope to finish Pet Rainbow and share it with the world, whether that’s in the form of gifts, performances, or an online business. I have plans to do some VJ sets, which is terrifying but also awesome. I’m interested in new ideas and collaboration, so if you want to connect, I would love to hear from you.

Happy Trails!

- Andy
